# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Owners and marketers at the "underserved middle" — boutique/SMB brands and public-sector/government teams (Section 508 buyers) — who are losing visibility as search shifts to AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews). Technical enough to respect a real engineering process, burned enough by agencies to distrust hype. They arrive skeptical and price-aware; they convert on credibility, not adjectives.

## Product Purpose
Prompt Goblin is a one-person AEO/GEO + technical-SEO + WCAG 2.1 AA / Section 508 accessibility shop. It makes brands citable by answer engines through an agentic, human-reviewed LangGraph pipeline that measures citation gaps and ships engineer-approved fixes. The marketing site sells the service and runs a free technical-hygiene scan as the top of the funnel. Success = a skeptical visitor runs the scan, believes the diagnosis, and books a Scout audit.

## Brand Personality
Feral-but-honest goblin voice over a terminal/HUD surface. Technically fluent, irreverent, allergic to marketing-speak. Three words: technical, irreverent, honest. The interface should feel like a competent engineer's terminal that happens to have a sense of humor — confidence earned by showing the actual system, never by claiming outcomes.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS landing pages: cream/beige body, soft gradients, hero-metric template, identical icon-card grids.
- Buzzword marketing (streamline / supercharge / unleash / world-class) and fabricated testimonials, client logos, or citation-count promises.
- "PDF audit" competitors who hand over a document and disappear.
- Editorial-magazine affectation (display-serif + italic + drop caps) — wrong register for a terminal-native dev brand.

## Design Principles
- **Honest broker.** Never show a number that wasn't measured. Schema is hygiene, not a citation lever. The refund guarantees the work, never a citation count.
- **Show the system, not a PDF.** Prove the pipeline on screen (real scans, real verdicts) instead of asserting capability.
- **Practice what you preach.** The site itself must be fast, accessible (AA/508), and citable — dogfooding is the proof.
- **Human-gated.** Nothing auto-ships; a software engineer reviews every change. The UI should make that accountability visible.
- **Terminal-native craft.** The dark + lime HUD aesthetic is the identity; restraint and precision over decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.1 AA / Section 508 is the product, so the site is held to it: axe-core 100/100, contrast ≥4.5:1 for small text (`--muted` 0.80, `--faint` 0.74 tokens are calibrated to clear it), visible focus, full keyboard support, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation. Custom cursor and motion degrade to native/static on touch and reduced-motion.
